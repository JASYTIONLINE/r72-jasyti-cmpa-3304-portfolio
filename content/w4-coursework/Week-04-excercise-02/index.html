KUK SOOL WON

The Traditional Martial Art of Korea

Kuk Sool Won is considered the Official Martial Art of Korea. The Korean word "KuK" directley translates to "NATION" but it is understood to mean "Korea" as well. "Sool" is "Technique." "WON" is "association." Therefore, KUK Sool Won translates into the Korean National Martial Arts Association.

A Medley of Poses (Goem)

Medley | 2025 | Techniques

Kuk Sool Won is a comprehensive system that includes striking, joint locks, throws, weapons, meditation, and healing techniques. It is a complete martial art that integrates both hard and soft styles, offering a balanced approach to training and self-defense. Here we see many of the different aspects of KSW.

Guard Position

Stance | 2024 | Offensive Guard

This stance may not look as impressive, but it is an important position in Kuk Sool Won. It is used for both offense and defense. From this position, you can quickly transition to other stances and techniques. It is not really considered a fighting stance but more of a ready position. In practice, we go low and wide to develop strength and stability. In real-world situations, we would stand more upright and relaxed, with the hands in a neutral, un-tensed position. This stance is not meant to signal aggression but rather to prepare for action if needed.

The Basic Front Kick

Technique | 2025 | Front Kick

Not bad for an old man! The front kick is one of the most fundamental techniques in Kuk Sool Won. It is used for both offense and defense. The kick is performed by lifting the knee and extending the leg forward to strike with the ball of the foot. It is a powerful technique that can target an opponent's midsection or head. The front kick is also useful for creating distance between you and your opponent, allowing you to set up other techniques. It is one of the fastest kicks in Kuk Sool Won, making it an effective tool for self-defense.

The Basic Side Kick

Technique | 2025 | Side Kick

The hips aren't what they used to be! Side kicks are a powerful technique in Kuk Sool Won. They are performed by lifting the knee and extending the leg to the side, striking with the heel or the edge of the foot. Side kicks are most effective for striking an opponent who is not directly in front of you. Side kicks require good balance and flexibility, making them a challenging technique to master. However, with practice they become a powerful tool for self-defense. It is not necessary to chamber the kick as high as you can; effective strikes can target the knee or ankle to disable an opponent, and these kicks can be used by elderly or mobility-limited practitioners.

Korean Straight Sword

Weapons | 2025 | Geom-Straight Sword

The Korean Straight Sword, also known as the "Geom," is a traditional weapon used in Kuk Sool Won. It is characterized by its straight, single or double-edged blade and is often used in forms and demonstrations. The sword techniques in Kuk Sool Won emphasize fluidity, precision, and control, making it a beautiful and effective weapon in martial arts practice. Fun Fact: I made this Sword 40 years ago.
